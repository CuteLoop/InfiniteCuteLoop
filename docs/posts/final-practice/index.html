33 Logistic Growth of Fish Population  {: #q33 }

Lake stocked with 1000 fish; model
\(P(t)=\dfrac{10\,000}{1+9e^{-t/5}}\), \(t\)=months. When will \(P=2000\)?

A. < 2 mo B. 2–4 mo C. 4–6 mo D. 6–8 mo E. > 8 mo

Solution \[ 2000 = \frac{10\,000}{1+9e^{-t/5}} \;\Longrightarrow\; 1+9e^{-t/5}=5 \;\Longrightarrow\; e^{-t/5}=4/9 \;\Longrightarrow\; t = 5\ln\!\bigl(\tfrac94\bigr) \approx 4.05\text{ mo}. \] Falls between 4 and 6 months ⇒ **Answer C**.

34 (***) Atmospheric‑Pressure Model  {: #q34 }

Pressure halves at 18 000 ft. Using \(y = Ce^{kt}\):

A. < 20 % B. 20–40 % C. 40–60 % D. 60–80 % E. > 80 %

Solution

  1. Half‑life equation

    \[ \tfrac12 = e^{k\cdot18\,000} \Longrightarrow k = \frac{\ln(\tfrac12)}{18\,000}\approx -3.85\times10^{-5}\,\text{ft}^{-1}. \]

  2. Interpretation
    \(k<0\) means pressure decreases exponentially with altitude.

  3. Pressure at 1000 ft

    \[ \frac{y}{C} = e^{k\cdot1000}=e^{-0.0385}\approx0.962, \]

    about 96 % of sea‑level pressure, so option E.
